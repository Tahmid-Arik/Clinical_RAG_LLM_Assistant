
DISEASE KNOWLEDGE BASE


## Data 01:- Common Cold (সর্দি-কাশি)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Gradual onset of a runny or stuffy nose, sneezing, a mild sore throat, a nagging cough, and watery eyes. A low-grade fever (100°F to 101°F or 37.8°C to 38.3°C) and mild body aches may be present, particularly in young children. Nasal discharge may start clear but naturally becomes thicker and yellow or green over a few days; this is a normal sign of the immune system working and does not automatically mean a bacterial infection has developed.
 * **Reasons:** An acute viral infection of the upper respiratory tract. While more than 200 different viruses can cause a cold, the **Rhinovirus** is the overwhelming culprit. The virus is highly contagious and spreads rapidly when an infected person coughs or sneezes, launching airborne respiratory droplets into the air. It also spreads through direct skin-to-skin contact or by touching contaminated everyday surfaces (like doorknobs or phones) and then touching your own eyes, nose, or mouth.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Because the common cold is viral, antibiotics are completely ineffective and should not be used. Treatment focuses entirely on easing symptoms while the body fights off the virus, which typically takes 7 to 10 days. Get plenty of rest and maximize your fluid intake. Over-the-counter (OTC) pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen can reduce fever and soothe a sore throat. Use saline nasal drops or sprays to loosen thick mucus, or use a cool-mist humidifier in the room to make breathing easier. Avoid giving OTC cough and cold medications to children under age 4 due to the risk of serious side effects.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For the Common Cold (সর্দি-কাশি) in Chattogram, isolate the patient as much as possible and practice strict handwashing to stop family transmission. Manage fever, body aches, and headaches safely using **Paracetamol**; avoid unnecessary OTC combination cold syrups without a doctor's guidance. Maintain hydration and soothe a raw throat cheaply using **Ginger-Honey Tea (আদা-মধু চা)**, warm **Saltwater Gargles (লবণ-গরম পানি)**, and fresh **Lemon-Zinc juice**, skipping costly imported immunity supplements. If the patient develops a persistent high fever (102°F+ or 38.9°C+ for more than 3 days), severe wheezing, difficulty breathing, an unyielding earache, or if a child refuses to feed and becomes unusually listless, seek immediate medical care at the outpatient or emergency wing of **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or consult a doctor instantly via the 24/7 government health hotline at **16263**.

## Data 02:- Chickenpox (জলবসন্ত)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** An intensely itchy skin rash that erupts into clusters of small, fluid-filled blisters resting on a red base (often described as "dewdrops on a rose petal"). The rash usually starts on the chest, back, or face before spreading across the entire body. It is accompanied by a fever, fatigue, a persistent headache, and a loss of appetite. The blisters eventually break open, ooze, and form crusty scabs.
 * **Reasons:** An acute respiratory infection caused by the highly contagious Varicella-zoster virus (VZV). The virus enters the body through the inhalation of airborne respiratory droplets or via direct contact with the fluid from the broken blisters of an infected person. Once contracted, it multiplies in the respiratory tract before spreading to the skin.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Treatment centers on soothing the intense itching and preventing secondary bacterial skin infections from scratching. Apply calamine lotion, give cool baths mixed with baking soda or colloidal oatmeal, and keep the child's fingernails trimmed short. Acetaminophen can be used to lower fever; strictly avoid ibuprofen and aspirin during chickenpox, as ibuprofen has been linked to severe, deep tissue skin infections, and aspirin can trigger Reye's syndrome. For individuals at high risk of complications, doctors may prescribe an antiviral medication like acyclovir if caught early. The definitive line of defense is prevention via the Varicella vaccine.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For Chickenpox (জলবসন্ত) in Chattogram, isolate the patient immediately and treat fever **only with Paracetamol**—strictly avoid Ibuprofen (linked to severe deep-tissue infections) and Aspirin (fatal Reye's syndrome risk). Soothe itching using calamine lotion, trimmed fingernails, and cool oatmeal or baking soda baths. Maintain hydration affordably with **Green Coconut Water (ডাবের পানি)**, **Lemon juice (লেবুর শরবত)**, and soft **Rice Gruel (জাউ ভাত)** instead of costly supplements. If the patient develops a persistent high fever, breathing difficulties, extreme lethargy, or if blisters become swollen, red, and filled with pus, seek immediate medical attention at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or call the 24/7 government health hotline at **16263**.

## Data 03:- Influenza (ইনফ্লুয়েঞ্জা / সিজনাল ফ্লু)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Unlike a common cold, influenza strikes with a sudden, abrupt onset of symptoms. It is characterized by a rapid spike in high fever (101°F to 104°F or 38.3°C to 40°C), severe and debilitating muscle or body aches, chills, and a persistent headache. This is accompanied by a dry, hacking cough, extreme and profound fatigue that can last for weeks, a sore throat, and a runny or stuffy nose.
 * **Reasons:** An acute, highly contagious respiratory infection caused by Influenza viruses (primarily types A and B). The virus spreads effortlessly through the air via microscopic respiratory droplets expelled when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. It can also be contracted by touching a surface contaminated with the virus and then touching the mouth, nose, or eyes. The virus infects the epithelial cells lining the respiratory tract, causing widespread inflammation and triggering a systemic immune response.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Antibiotics are completely useless against influenza because it is a viral infection. Management centers on strict bed rest and heavy fluid consumption to prevent dehydration. Over-the-counter antipyretics like acetaminophen or ibuprofen are highly effective for controlling fever and body aches. For individuals at high risk of severe complications (such as the elderly, young children, pregnant women, or those with asthma), a doctor may prescribe an antiviral medication like oseltamivir, which works best if started within 48 hours of symptom onset. The premier line of defense is an annual seasonal flu vaccine.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For Influenza (ফ্লু) in Chattogram, enforce immediate home isolation and ensure the patient uses a separate room and mask to prevent spreading the virus to high-risk family members. Control the high fever and severe muscle pain strictly with **Paracetamol**; stay well-hydrated without expensive energy drinks by using **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS / খাবার স্যালাইন)**, fresh **Malta juice**, and hot **Chicken or Vegetable Broth (গরম স্যুপ)**. If the patient develops a sudden drop in oxygen levels, shortness of breath, chest pain, a fever that returns after initially disappearing, or severe confusion/lethargy, seek immediate emergency medical care at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or seek urgent advice via the 24/7 government medical helpline at **16263**.

## Data 04:- COVID-19 (করোনাভাইরাস)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Symptoms vary widely but characteristically feature a fever, a persistent dry cough, shortness of breath or breathing difficulties, and profound fatigue. A unique, hallmark diagnostic sign is the sudden loss of taste (ageusia) or smell (anosmia). Other common traits include a sore throat, severe headaches, muscle or body aches, chills, and gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea or nausea. Symptoms typically appear 2 to 14 days after exposure to the virus.
 * **Reasons:** An infectious respiratory disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus 2). The virus is highly contagious and spreads primarily through respiratory droplets and microscopic aerosols generated when an infected person breathes, talks, coughs, or sneezes in close proximity to others. It can also spread in poorly ventilated indoor settings where the virus remains suspended in the air. The virus enters human cells by binding to ACE2 receptors, primarily in the respiratory tract, leading to localized tissue damage and a systemic inflammatory immune response.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Most mild cases can be managed safely at home with strict isolation, rest, and plenty of fluids. Over-the-counter pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen can be used to manage fever and body aches. For individuals at high risk of progressing to severe disease due to age or underlying conditions, antiviral medications (such as Paxlovid) may be prescribed by a doctor if initiated early. Monitor blood oxygen saturation (SpO2) regularly using a pulse oximeter. Prevention is heavily driven by staying up to date with recommended vaccine boosters, improving indoor ventilation, and wearing high-quality masks in crowded spaces.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For COVID-19 in Chattogram, place the patient in immediate single-room isolation and ensure all household members wear surgical or N95 masks. Manage fever and mild body pain safely using **Paracetamol**; strictly avoid self-medicating with unprescribed antibiotics (like azithromycin) or steroids. Maintain essential hydration and electrolyte balances affordably using **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS / খাবার স্যালাইন)**, **Green Coconut Water (ডাবের পানি)**, and warm **Ginger-Black Tea (রং চা)**. If the patient's pulse oximeter reading drops below 92%, or if they experience sudden chest pressure, severe shortness of breath, or blue-tinted lips, transport them immediately to the specialized isolation and respiratory care wings at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or call the 24/7 national health hotline at **16263** for urgent ambulance coordination and bed availability.

## Data 05:- Typhoid Fever (টাইফয়েড)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Characterized by a sustained, step-ladder fever that climbs higher day by day, potentially reaching 103°F to 104°F (39.4°C to 40°C). This is accompanied by severe weakness, stomach pain, a persistent headache, a dry cough, and a distinct loss of appetite. A unique sign can be a flat, rose-colored spotty rash on the chest or abdomen. It frequently causes severe constipation or "pea-soup" liquid diarrhea, along with significant abdominal bloating.
 * **Reasons:** A life-threatening systemic bacterial infection caused by the bacterium Salmonella enterica serovar Typhi. The bacteria live exclusively in humans and are shed through the feces and urine of infected individuals or chronic, asymptomatic carriers. It spreads via the fecal-oral route through the consumption of contaminated water or food that has been handled by someone shedding the bacteria. Once ingested, the bacteria multiply in the intestinal mucosa, enter the bloodstream, and invade organs like the liver, spleen, and bone marrow.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Requires prompt antibiotic therapy prescribed by a doctor following confirmation via blood culture or a Widal test. Completing the entire prescribed course of antibiotics (such as azithromycin, ceftriaxone, or ciprofloxacin) is absolutely vital to prevent a relapse or becoming a chronic carrier, even if symptoms vanish early. Resting in bed, eating soft, easily digestible foods, and consuming continuous fluids to replace what is lost to fever and diarrhea are essential supportive measures. Prevention centers on strict food safety and getting vaccinated.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For Typhoid Fever in Chattogram, practice strict bathroom hygiene and handwashing to protect family members from fecal-oral transmission. Manage the high fever safely using **Paracetamol**; never take aspirin or heavy NSAIDs as typhoid increases the risk of intestinal bleeding. Maintain hydration and caloric baseline affordably using **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS / খাবার স্যালাইন)**, fresh **Green Coconut Water (ডাবের পানি)**, and warm, well-strained **Barley water or Rice Gruel (জাউ ভাত)**. If the patient develops sudden, severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting of blood, a rigid/swollen stomach, or extreme confusion, seek immediate emergency hospitalization at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)** or **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)** to treat potential intestinal perforation, or call **16263** for urgent guidance.

## Data 06:- Cholera (কলেরা)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Characterized by an abrupt, sudden onset of painless, massive watery diarrhea that resembles "rice-water" (flecks of white mucus in a clear/cloudy liquid). The stool loss is rapid and severe, often accompanied by forceful vomiting and intense muscle cramps in the legs due to rapid salt depletion. This causes extreme, life-threatening dehydration within hours, marked by sunken eyes, dry mouth, cold/clammy skin, severe lethargy, and wrinkled skin on the fingers ("washerwoman's hands").
 * **Reasons:** An acute diarrheal infection caused by ingestion of food or water contaminated with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. The bacteria do not invade the intestinal tissue directly; instead, they colonize the small intestine and release a potent enterotoxin called cholera toxin. This toxin binds to the intestinal walls and forces the body to rapidly pump massive amounts of water, sodium, chloride, and potassium out of the bloodstream and into the bowel, causing explosive, catastrophic fluid loss.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Immediate, aggressive fluid and electrolyte replacement is the gold standard of care and saves lives. Mild to moderate cases are treated using massive quantities of standard Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS). Severe cases require immediate intravenous (IV) fluids like Cholera Saline (rehydration fluid) in a clinical environment. Short-course antibiotics (such as doxycycline or azithromycin) can be prescribed by a doctor to reduce the duration of diarrhea and decrease bacterial shedding, but fluids remain the primary lifesaver.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For suspected Cholera in Chattogram, do not wait for a clinic appointment—begin drinking Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS / খাবার স্যালাইন) immediately at home, taking a sip after every loose stool. Use clean, boiled water to mix the ORS. Maintain electrolyte support affordably with **ORS and Green Coconut Water (ডাবের পানি)**, entirely skipping solid foods until vomiting stops. If the patient cannot keep fluids down, loses consciousness, or exhibits rapid, weak pulses, bypass local pharmacies and transport them immediately to the diarrheal isolation unit at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or contact the government helpline at **16263** for emergency stabilization protocols.

## Data 07:- Tuberculosis (যক্ষ্মা)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Characterized by a persistent, hacking cough that lasts for more than three weeks, which frequently brings up thick sputum or blood (hemoptysis). This is accompanied by chronic low-grade fever that typically peaks in the evening, severe night sweats that drench clothes, unexplained weight loss ("consumption"), a total loss of appetite, persistent chest pain when breathing or coughing, and profound, ongoing fatigue.
 * **Reasons:** A highly infectious disease caused by the rod-shaped bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis. The bacteria primarily target the lungs (pulmonary TB) but can travel through the bloodstream to infect other organs like the kidneys, spine, or brain (extrapulmonary TB). It is an airborne disease that spreads when a person with active pulmonary TB coughs, sneezes, speaks, or spits, launching microscopic droplets containing the bacteria into the air for others to inhale. It is not spread by sharing utensils or shaking hands.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Requires a strict, long-term combination course of multiple antibiotics managed via Directly Observed Therapy (DOTS) to prevent the rise of Drug-Resistant TB (MDR-TB). Standard treatment lasts 6 to 9 months and utilizes a first-line four-drug regimen: isoniazid, rifampin, ethambutol, and pyrazinamide. Never skip or stop taking these medications early, even if you feel completely healthy, as the bacteria can mutate and return in a highly dangerous, untreatable form. Rest, proper ventilation, and high-protein nutrition are crucial supportive measures.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For suspected Tuberculosis in Chattogram, go immediately to a government-designated DOTS center for a free Sputum Test or GeneXpert DNA test and a Chest X-ray. All TB testing and long-term medications are completely free under the Bangladesh National Tuberculosis Control Program (NTP). Maintain essential strength and muscle recovery affordably using local high-protein foods like **Eggs (ডিম)**, **Lentil Soup (ডালের ডাল/স্যুপ)**, and fresh seasonal fruits instead of commercial protein powders. If the patient coughs up massive amounts of blood, experiences severe chest pain, or struggles to breathe, go directly to the chest disease or respiratory wing at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, the **Chittagong Chest Disease Hospital (বক্ষব্যাধি হাসপাতাল) in Faujdarhat**, or call **16263** for immediate guidance.

## Data 08:- Malaria (ম্যালেরিয়া)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Characterized by recurrent, cyclic attacks of high fever (103°F to 105°F or 39.4°C to 40.5°C) that follow a highly distinct three-stage pattern: a cold stage (uncontrollable shivering and severe chills), a hot stage (high fever, severe headache, intense muscle aches, and vomiting), and a sweating stage (profuse sweating as the fever abruptly drops back to normal). These cycles typically repeat every 48 to 72 hours depending on the specific parasite strain.
 * **Reasons:** A life-threatening blood infection caused by microscopic protozoan parasites belonging to the genus Plasmodium (most dangerously Plasmodium falciparum and Plasmodium vivax). The parasite is transmitted to humans through the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito, which typically bites between dusk and dawn. Once inside the human body, the parasites travel directly to the liver to mature and multiply, after which they enter the bloodstream and infect and destroy red blood cells, causing severe anemia and widespread organ inflammation.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Requires urgent medical diagnosis via a Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT) or a thick/thin blood smear under a microscope, followed by immediate prescription antimalarial therapy. The gold standard treatment for P. falciparum is Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT), while P. vivax requires a combination of chloroquine and primaquine to entirely clear the parasites hiding in the liver. Complete the full medical course exactly as prescribed to avoid fatal complications or relapses.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** Malaria is highly endemic in the hill tract regions neighboring Chattogram (such as Rangamati, Bandarban, and Khagrachhari). If you develop a cycling high fever after traveling to these areas, get tested immediately. Testing and antimalarial medications are provided completely free by the Bangladesh government's National Malaria Elimination Program at upazila health complexes. Manage fever safely using **Paracetamol**; avoid heavy NSAIDs. Maintain cellular hydration and energy cheaply using **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS / খাবার স্যালাইন)**, local **Green Coconut Water (ডাবের পানি)**, and warm **Rice Gruel (জাউ ভাত)**. If the patient develops signs of severe malaria—such as extreme jaundice (yellowing of eyes/skin), dark-colored urine, convulsions, breathing distress, or loss of consciousness (cerebral malaria)—transport them immediately to the emergency or medicine units of **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or call **16263** for urgent critical care routing.

## Data 09:- Pneumonia (নিউমোনিয়া)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Characterized by a deep, painful cough that produces thick, discolored mucus (which may be green, yellow, or tinged with blood). It features a high fever, shaking chills, and noticeable shortness of breath or rapid, shallow breathing. A hallmark sign is sharp, stabbing chest pain that intensifies significantly when breathing deeply or coughing. In infants and young children, look for widening nostrils (nasal flaring) and the skin pulling in deeply between or below the ribs during inhalation (intercostal retractions).
 * **Reasons:** An acute respiratory infection that causes inflammation in the alveoli (the microscopic, balloon-like air sacs) in one or both lungs. Triggered by a variety of pathogens, including bacteria (most commonly Streptococcus pneumoniae), viruses (like RSV or influenza), or fungi. When the pathogen invades the respiratory system, the body's immune response causes the alveoli to fill up with pus, inflammatory cells, and cellular fluid. This fluid buildup severely blocks the normal exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide, making breathing difficult and lowering blood oxygen levels.
 * **Treatment Advice:** Bacterial pneumonia requires a prompt, full course of prescription antibiotics (such as amoxicillin or azithromycin) directed by a doctor. Viral pneumonia does not respond to antibiotics and is managed with supportive care or specific antiviral medications if indicated. Never stop taking prescribed antibiotics early, even if symptoms improve, to prevent a severe relapse or antibiotic resistance. Use acetaminophen to control fever, rest strictly, and use a cool-mist humidifier or steam inhalation to loosen chest mucus. Monitor blood oxygen saturation (SpO2) continuously with a pulse oximeter.
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For suspected Pneumonia in Chattogram, closely monitor the patient’s breathing rate and chest movement. Control fever safely using **Paracetamol**; avoid heavy, unprescribed cough suppressants that prevent the lungs from clearing fluid. Keep the respiratory tract hydrated and soothe chest discomfort cheaply using warm **Tulsi-Ginger Tea (তুলসি-আদা চা)**, clear **Homemade Vegetable or Chicken Broth**, and plenty of warm water. If the patient’s oxygen level drops below 92%, or if an infant develops a grunting sound, refuses to feed, or shows visible chest retractions, bypass local medicine stalls and go immediately to the respiratory or pediatric emergency wings at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH)**, **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**, or call **16263** for urgent ambulance assistance and oxygen support.

## Data 10:- Dengue Fever (ডেঙ্গু)
 * **Identifying Characteristics:** Characterized by a sudden, explosive onset of high fever (102°F to 104°F or 39°C to 40°C) accompanied by a severe, blinding headache and a highly distinct pain behind the eyes (retro-orbital pain). It features excruciating muscle and joint aches, earning it the historical name "breakbone fever." Within 2 to 5 days after the fever starts, a flat, patchy red skin rash may appear across the body. It can cause severe nausea, persistent vomiting, and mild bleeding traits like swollen, oozing gums or easy bruising.
 * **Reasons:** An acute viral infection caused by the Dengue virus (DENV), which exists as four distinct but closely related serotypes (DENV-1, -2, -3, and -4). The virus is transmitted to humans through the bite of an infected female Aedes mosquito (primarily Aedes aegypti), which breeds preferentially in clean, stagnant water around residential areas and bites mostly during daylight hours. Once inside the bloodstream, the virus replicates and causes temporary bone marrow suppression and a systemic inflammatory response that increases capillary permeability, causing fluids to leak out of the blood vessels.
 * **Treatment Advice:** There is no specific antiviral medication or cure for dengue; treatment is entirely supportive and focuses on strict fluid management and pain control. **Take only Paracetamol (Acetaminophen) for fever and pain.** Rest completely in bed, track your daily urine output, and consume massive amounts of oral fluids to counteract plasma leakage. Monitoring a Complete Blood Count (CBC) daily to track platelet counts and hematocrit (PCV) levels is absolutely mandatory as the fever begins to drop, which marks the entrance into the dangerous "critical phase."
 * **Local Action Protocol (Bangladesh):** For Dengue in Chattogram, get a Dengue NS1 Antigen test and a CBC on Day 1 to 3 of fever. If platelets drop below 100,000, or if the patient exhibits any warning signs—such as severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding from the gums/nose, or extreme lethargy—transport them immediately for hospitalization at **Chittagong Medical College Hospital (CMCH) (Medicine Wards 14, 15, or 16)** or **Chittagong General Hospital (Anderkilla)**. Maintain crucial electrolyte balances affordably using **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS / খাবার স্যালাইন)**, fresh **Green Coconut Water (ডাবের পানি)**, and local **Lemon juice (লেবুর শরবত)** with a pinch of salt. For immediate government medical advice or blood bank coordination, call **16263** or **333**.

*Absolute Safety Restriction:** *
*NEVER take Ibuprofen, Diclofenac, Naproxen, or Aspirin (NSAIDs)** for Dengue fever. These medications thin the blood further and can trigger catastrophic, fatal internal bleeding.
